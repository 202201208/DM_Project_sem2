Image Encryption

Using Chaotic Functions & DNA Encoding

Image encryption using chaotic functions and DNA encoding is a secure method for protecting digital images. Chaotic functions generate pseudorandom numbers as encryption keys, scrambling the pixels and making decryption without the correct keys nearly impossible. DNA encoding adds an extra layer of security by representing the encrypted image as a DNA sequence, making it even more complex to decipher. This technique ensures confidentiality in image transmission, safeguarding sensitive information in sectors like healthcare and military.

Image Steganography

Image steganography is a technique used to hide secret information within an image without arousing suspicion. It involves embedding the hidden data within the pixels of the image, making it imperceptible to the human eye. By exploiting the redundant information and the limitations of human visual perception, steganography ensures that the hidden data remains concealed during transmission or storage. Our method outputs png format image because jpg or jpeg are compressed image.

Image Processing

Image processing encompasses various techniques such as enhancement, transformation, tranoise reduction, edge detection, and image embossing. These techniques play a vital role in improving image quality, reducing noise artifacts, detecting boundaries, and adding visual effects to digital images, enabling a wide range of applications in fields like computer vision, medical imaging, and multimedia processing.
